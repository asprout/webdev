Predicting success

A startup is a small, young company that is intended to grow rapidly and to become large and influential. They are typically backed by venture capital (V.C.) firms. The people who create startups are called founders. A "unicorn" is a startup that has achieved a valuation of 1 billion dollars or more.

Some people believe that a startup is more likely to become a unicorn if it is founded by someone who has dropped out of college. Here are some examples of unicorns founded by college dropouts: Facebook (Mark Zuckerberg), Twitter (Jack Dorsey), Uber (Travis Kalanick).

In your best estimate, out of the 10,000 V.C. backed companies with founders who dropped out of college, how many do you think became unicorns?
